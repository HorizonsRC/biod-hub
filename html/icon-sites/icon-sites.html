Icon Site · Whanganui
Bushy Park Tarapuruhi

An 89 ha predator-proof fenced forest sanctuary northwest of Whanganui, protecting a rare remnant of temperate lowland bush effectively free of pest mammals since 2005. Owned and partially funded by Forest and Bird, managed by Bushy Park Trust. Home to reintroduced hihi, tīeke, toutouwai, popokatea and tītipounamu.

Horizons Involvement

Provision of annual funding to support their biodiversity protection activities. Biodiversity staff representation on the Forest Committee and providing general advice and support where their technical knowledge and skills are useful. Bushy Park is classed as one of the actively managed sites in the Priority Habitats programme. Biodiversity advisors carry out a rapid ecological assessment of the forest every 5 years.

Icon Site · Manawatū Gorge
Te Āpiti Manawatū

Around 2,700 ha of forested gorge between Woodville and Ashhurst, encompassing the Northern and Southern Te Āpiti Scenic Reserves, adjacent public domains and the Manawatū River. A well-used recreational hub corridor attracting new developments guided by a 2022 Masterplan. The project is managed through a broad multi-agency Governance Group.

Horizons Involvement

Councillor representation and chairing of the governance group. Biodiversity team facilitating meetings and reporting, overseeing the project budget and working with DOC and contractors to improve forest health. Biosecurity pest animal team servicing traps and PCO bait stations in the buffer zones. Communications team assisting with marketing, communications and social media presence for the project.

Icon Site · Mt Ruapehu Foothills
Kia Whārite Biodiversity Project

A landscape-scale predator and pest control project that has been running since 2008, over 190,000+ hectares west and northwest of Mt Ruapehu. Targets for North Island brown kiwi population growth of 10% per year and 50 whio pairs on the Retaruke and Manganui-o-te-ao rivers have both been achieved and sustained.

Horizons Involvement

The Biosecurity pest plant and pest animal teams carrying out pest plant and possum control on private land within the project areas. The biodiversity team administering the project budget and managing contracts for predator trap servicing on the Retaruke River and in the Waimarino Forest sites. Coordinating collaboratively with DOC and project stakeholders.

Icon Site · Foxton Beach
Manawatū Estuary

A 558 ha Ramsar-listed estuary at Foxton Beach with internationally recognised biodiversity values. Stewardship rests with the MEMT — a broad coalition of DOC, councils, iwi and community organisations, guided by the 2025–2035 Papangaio Te Wharangi Manawatu Estuary Management Plan.

Horizons Involvement

Horizons biodiversity team providing advice, meeting facilitation and funding toward pest plant and pest animal control. The Biosecurity pest animal team servicing predator traps in the Fernbird Flat area.

Icon Site · Eketāhuna
Pūkaha Mt Bruce

A 942 ha unfenced wildlife sanctuary near Eketāhuna, straddling the Horizons–Wellington boundary. Features a visitor centre, education programmes and captive breeding for kōkako, kiwi, takahē, kākā, whio and kākāriki.

Horizons Involvement

Horizons' biodiversity team administering funding for biodiversity protection work and overseeing pest plant control in the buffer. The Biosecurity pest animal team servicing traps in the northern buffer area around Pūkaha.

Icon Site · Southern Ruahine
Southern Ruahine Kiwi

Launched in 2022, this community-led partnership between ENM's Source to Sea Collective and Te Kāuru Hapū Collective aims to reintroduce North Island eastern brown kiwi through a 23,000 ha mustelid trap network across the Southern Ruahine Ranges.

Horizons Involvement

Provision of annual funding to support their operations. Horizons biodiversity and biosecurity pest animal staff providing technical advice and support for the project. Councillor representation on governance group.
